We are human. As we live, we eventually figure out we are going to die. This motivates us to find meaning and distraction. (The key components of art.) We also make little copies of ourselves and compete for wealth and power by predicting the future.

Most living things try to predict the future and influence events. Humans do this by modeling the world. In each of our heads is a simplified version of the world that we generated from millions and millions of interactions with the real world (learning). When we want to know what will happen, we consult our model, we use our imagination to simulate reality.

We sometimes import other peoples models by going to college or watching YouTube. We sometimes export our models by explaining ballistics to another person.

(((Ballistics example, analytical)))

- Newtons Laws
- Free-body diagram
- ball moves in an almost parabolic arc
- motivated by "How high should I aim my cannon to best kill those people over there?"

Exporting and importing models (human communication) is itself a pretty strange and complicated phenomenon. The success of homo sapiens is largely attributed to the fact that we are very good at communication, but it is still a major limitation. We "download" slow compared to computers.

For us, modeling and simulation are automatic. We do it without thinking or meaning to. We compute likely outcomes given imaginary actions. But, with computers we can make models and simulations explicit in code and offload the computation to a machine.
